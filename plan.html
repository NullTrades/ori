the money format

Repostable list-dumps

Photo dump + text on screen, framed as a relatable list — "things I'm saying no to," "things I find attractive." This is the one that gets reposted to people's stories, which is how it reaches audiences you'd never touch. It also merges your Takes voice with your proven dump format. One rule above all: specific + a little spicy + your voice. Generic ("confidence, ambition, kindness") dies on arrival; oddly specific ("people who actually text back") gets sent to three friends.

  • Cover = bait, not your best photo. Its job is the title + stopping the scroll. Keep a real payoff line for the last slide so people swipe to the end — that's what re-serves it to non-followers.
  • Pair photo to line where you can; otherwise keep the photos cohesive so it still feels like an effortless dump, not a stiff slideshow.
  • Caption invites the repost: "send this to whoever needs to hear it," "save the reminder," "what's on your list 👇."
  • Build once, post twice: the same set becomes a carousel (saves + conversion) and a reel with trending audio + the text (cold reach). One idea, both jobs.

04 — the look

Lock it once, never decide again

A "branded" feel is just the same look every single time. Build these once into a Premiere template and a matching CapCut preset so both outputs are identical. This is what makes a feed read as one creator.

a Captions / subtitles

  • Font: SF Pro Display is your pick — it lives under device fonts in iOS CapCut. Install it on the Mac (Apple's developer font pack) so Premiere matches exactly. Set one CapCut fallback and never touch it again.
  • Position: centered, lower third, same size every time.
  • Style: white fill, subtle dark stroke or soft shadow so it reads on any background. Burn captions in — don't rely on auto-captions (boosts sound-off watch time).
  • Accent: one highlight colour for emphasis words, the same hue forever. It becomes part of your brand.
  • Animation: one style — word-by-word karaoke highlight or a clean pop-in. Pick it, lock it.

b Colour grade

  • You shoot Rec.709 — a clean neutral base. Build one signature grade (suggestion: gently warm, lifted shadows, soft contrast — flattering on skin, cosy-premium feel).
  • Premiere: save it as a LUT + Lumetri preset; drop on every clip.
  • CapCut: recreate as a saved Filter + Adjustment preset. Same white balance, same exposure target across phone and desktop.

c Premiere template & covers

  • Extend your existing template: saved caption style, saved LUT, export preset (1080×1920, H.264, ~15–20 Mbps), audio normalised to about −14 LUFS so every video sounds the same.
  • One reel-cover template — cover text in your locked font, same position, same colour, reused every time. Your grid is your conversion page; consistent covers are what flip a profile visitor into a follower.
05 — the test engine

Trial reels (live for you now)

You've got the toggle, so this runs from day one — your way to test without ever risking the grid.

  • What it does: posts a reel to non-followers only; after a ~24–72h window you get data and can share winners to your feed (or let Instagram auto-share if it clears the threshold).
  • Run 2–3 trials a week testing genuinely new ideas — separate from your scheduled feed reels. Think of them as auditions.
  • Share only the winners to your real feed. The grid stays a highlight reel of bangers.
  • Compare trials only to other trials — they get less reach by design (no follower head-start), so judging them against normal reels misleads you. An 800-view trial can be a winner.
  • Never re-upload old content as a trial — 2026 penalises reposts platform-wide.
  • Change one variable per trial (a hook, a pillar, talking-head vs concept). Let strangers vote, then promote winners into next week's feed slots.

07 — make them stay

Hooks, captions & the reason to follow

Hooks — first 1–3 seconds

Open a loop they have to stay to close, in your voice: "The way people style this is a crime and I'm pressing charges…" / "Unpopular opinion but…" / "POV: you asked me about this so here's the unhinged truth…" Lead with the payoff, not a slow intro.

Captions = SEO, not hashtags

Write keyword-rich captions in natural language — say what it's about in words people search. 3–5 hashtags max. End on a soft open loop, never "follow for more": "part 2 tmrw," "tell me I'm wrong," "which one's worse 👇."

Name your series

A random funny take is a view; "episode 4 of the bit they're waiting for" is a follow. Name your recurring formats so people anticipate them — a series is a promise, and a promise is a reason to follow. End each with a teaser for the next.
